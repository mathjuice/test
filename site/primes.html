Primality Tester

For a positive integer \(n\) define a function $$ \tau(n) = \sum_{d | n} 1 $$ which gives the number of positive divisors of \(n \). A positive integer \(p\) is called prime if and only if \(\tau(p) =2\). A number \(n\) is composite if it can be factored as \(n=xy\) where \(x\) and \(y\) are not equal to one. A number that is composite is not prime.

Enter a positive integer to determine whether or not it is prime!
